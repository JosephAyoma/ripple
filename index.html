About Ripple-EDD

Ripple Education Drivers is a leading nonprofit initiative driving transformative education across East Africa. We are dedicated to fostering learner-centered education by championing models such as the Competency-Based Curriculum (CBC). Our core belief is that educational transformation begins with informed and engaged communities. We work collaboratively with parents, educators, policy influencers, and civil society to demystify education reforms, clarify the objectives of CBC, and provide platforms for dialogue, collaboration, and shared learning. By bridging knowledge gaps and fostering partnerships, we help build inclusive, future-ready education systems that support every learner's growth and potential. Through community outreach, professional development, digital engagement, and policy dialogue, Ripple Education Drivers is catalyzing a movement where education becomes a shared responsibility — and a catalyst for societal progress.

Dr. Clara Chao Igobwa
Dr. Clara Chao Igobwa is a seasoned educator with extensive experience in teaching and educational leadership. Her teaching career spans all levels of education; from kindergarten to university - demonstrating her deep commitment to learner development at every stage. Driven by a passion for improving the educational experience, Dr. Clara actively works to engage and empower key stakeholders, including parents, teachers, and the broader community. She is dedicated to sharing practical knowledge and insights that enable these groups to better support learners and promote effective, inclusive learning environments. As co-founder and Lead Coach at Ripple Ed, Dr. Clara leverages her vast expertise to inspire a positive ripple effect in education across East Africa and beyond. Through her leadership, she aims to foster meaningful, lasting improvements in educational practice and learner outcomes throughout the region.
Jane Smith
Jane is a dedicated educator with a strong background in learner-centered methodologies and digital instruction. She leads Ripple-EDD’s professional development programs.
Faith Marube
Faith Marube, popularly known as Sonya, is a dedicated educator with a passion for facilitating accessible and practical approaches to learning. Her experience includes both classroom environments and online platforms, providing her with valuable insight into how education can be effectively delivered across diverse settings and tailored to meet the needs of different learners. In addition to her work in education, Faith has actively participated in community initiatives and has gained experience in sectors such as construction. These diverse engagements have built in her a spirit of adaptability, open-mindedness, and the ability to connect meaningfully with individuals from varied backgrounds. As the Co-founder of Ripple Education Drivers, Faith is committed to equipping parents, educators, and community stakeholders with a deeper understanding of the evolving education landscape, particularly in relation to the implementation of the Competency-Based Curriculum (CBC). She firmly believes that an informed community is better positioned to support learners effectively and to contribute towards building a more promising and inclusive future for all.
